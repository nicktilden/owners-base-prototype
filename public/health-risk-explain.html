02 / The Problem
Owners
are
responsible.
But blind.

As a result, Owners don't know where to focus their attention and struggle to clearly identify risk and understand root cause.

The Problem
Owners are responsible for portfolio outcomes, but they can't see the full picture.

Owners are responsible for the performance and outcomes of an entire portfolio, yet today they are forced to piece together signals from individual projects, tools, and reports to understand risk.

Risk is identified too late
By the time a project is flagged, corrective action is already constrained.
Risk is buried in project detail
Portfolio-level visibility requires manually aggregating across dozens of projects.
Risk is subjective and inconsistent
Risk severity varies by PM, team, and region — making comparison impossible.
Risk is communicated reactively
Status reaches leadership through weekly reports and meetings, not live signals.
The Opportunity
Every customer has invented a workaround.
None of them have a purpose-built solution.

06 / Object Model
Three
named
objects.
One
architecture.

Understanding the distinction between these three objects is the architectural foundation. Each lives at a different scope, serves a different purpose, and is created differently.

Object hierarchy
Risk Type
Account level
The admin-defined template. Defines what a risk looks like — fields, scoring behavior, health dimension mapping, default status lifecycle, and compliance framing.
Financial Schedule Safety Quality Regulatory Site Contractual
Managed in Company Settings → Health & Risk → Risk Types
Risk Record
Project level
One record tracking one uncertainty or issue across its full lifecycle — from identification to resolution. Created manually or promoted from an automated signal.
Risk Records are not raw source data. They are a distinct input type — human-entered or promoted — and their completeness is not guaranteed.
Risk Signal
Company level
The derived aggregation of Risk Records at the company level. Not a new object — a computed view. Clicking a Signal opens a filtered list of existing Risk Records across projects.
Risk Signal → click opens filtered Risk Records across all projects

07 / Architecture
Four
layers.
Two
paths.

The system is a pipeline from raw source data to the experience layer. Two calculation paths run simultaneously — current-state and forecast — and return through the same result type.

View Demo →
System Pipeline
1 — Source Data
Budget / Financial Change Events Schedule & Milestones Risk Records RFIs / Submittals Invoices Funding Data
Risk Records are distinct from tool data — completeness is not guaranteed.
2 — Normalization
Standardize units Validate completeness Data integrity branch Risk Record origin flag
Incomplete or stale data routes to the integrity branch — produces a degraded score, never suppressed.
3 — Calculation
Current-state path Forecast path Threshold & rules engine Portfolio roll-up
Both paths run simultaneously. Current: tool data → health signals. Forecast: Risk Records → probability × impact → expected exposure.
4 — Experience
KPI Cards Portfolio hub Project hub KPI Wizard Health tearsheet
Trust, Transparency & Action — every signal explained, every threshold editable, every risk actionable.
